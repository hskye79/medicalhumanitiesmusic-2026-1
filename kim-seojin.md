# Rent

Premiered in 1996, *Rent* was written, composed, and scripted by Jonathan Larson (1960–1996), winning the Pulitzer Prize for Drama and the Tony Award for Best Musical. Set in New York's East Village in the early 1990s, the story follows Mark, a struggling filmmaker, and Roger, a musician, living in a freezing apartment. Roger, dealing with the suicide of his former lover and his own HIV diagnosis, struggles to write one last great song before he dies. Their friend Collins meets Angel, a drag queen, and they fall in love, while Roger tentatively begins a relationship with Mimi. Most of the characters live with HIV, a constant existential threat. Through the death of Angel and the survival of others, the musical vividly portrays the affirmation of life through the philosophy of "No Day But Today."
